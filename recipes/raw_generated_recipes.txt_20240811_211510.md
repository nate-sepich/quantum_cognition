## 5 High-Protein, High-Carb Recipes (Under 30 Minutes)

These recipes utilize your inventory and meet your macro goals of approximately 190g protein, 477g carbs, and 126g fat.  

**Recipe 1: Chicken & Quinoa Burrito Bowl**

* **Prep Time:** 25 minutes
* **Ingredients:**
    * 1 Chicken Breast (cooked and shredded)
    * 1/2 cup Quinoa (cooked)
    * 1/2 cup Black Beans (canned, rinsed)
    * 1/2 cup Corn (frozen or canned)
    * 1/4 cup Salsa
    * 1/4 cup Greek Yogurt
    * 1 tbsp Olive Oil
    * Spices: Chili powder, cumin, paprika to taste

* **Instructions:**
    1. While the quinoa cooks, heat olive oil in a pan and cook the chicken breast (seasoned with spices) until cooked through. Shred the chicken.
    2. In a bowl, combine cooked quinoa, black beans, corn, salsa, and spices. 
    3. Top with shredded chicken and a dollop of Greek yogurt.

**Recipe 2: Salmon with Roasted Broccoli & Brown Rice**

* **Prep Time:** 25 minutes
* **Ingredients:** 
    * 1 Salmon Fillet 
    * 1 cup Broccoli florets
    * 1/2 cup Brown Rice (cooked)
    * 1 tbsp Olive Oil
    * Lemon wedges (for serving)
    * Salt and pepper to taste

* **Instructions:** 
    1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
    2. Toss broccoli florets with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Spread on a baking sheet.
    3. Place salmon fillet on the same baking sheet. Season with salt and pepper.
    4. Roast for 15-20 minutes, or until salmon is cooked through and broccoli is tender.
    5. Serve salmon and broccoli with a side of cooked brown rice. Squeeze lemon juice over the salmon before serving.

**Recipe 3: Chicken and Spinach Scramble with Sweet Potato Toast**

* **Prep Time:** 20 minutes
* **Ingredients:**
    * 1 Chicken Breast (cooked and diced)
    * 1 cup Spinach 
    * 4 Eggs
    * 1 Sweet Potato (sliced into "toast")
    * 1/2 cup Greek Yogurt
    * 1 tbsp Olive Oil
    * Salt and pepper to taste

* **Instructions:**
    1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Toss sweet potato slices with olive oil, salt, and pepper and bake for 15-20 minutes, flipping halfway through.
    2. While the sweet potato cooks, heat a pan over medium heat and scramble the eggs with salt and pepper. 
    3. Add the diced chicken and spinach to the pan and cook until spinach is wilted.
    4. Serve the scramble alongside the sweet potato toast. Top with a dollop of Greek Yogurt.

**Recipe 4: Almond Butter & Banana Protein Pancakes**

* **Prep Time:** 15 minutes
* **Ingredients:**
    * 1/2 cup Oats (blended into flour)
    * 1 scoop Protein Powder (vanilla or unflavored)
    * 1 Banana (mashed)
    * 2 Eggs
    * 2 tbsp Almond Butter
    * 1/4 cup Milk (any kind)
    * 1/4 cup Blueberries 

* **Instructions:**
    1. Blend oats in a blender until they reach a fine flour consistency.
    2. In a bowl, combine oat flour, protein powder, mashed banana, eggs, almond butter, and milk. Mix well.
    3. Heat a lightly oiled griddle or pan over medium heat. Pour 1/4 cup of batter per pancake onto the griddle. 
    4. Cook for 2-3 minutes per side, or until golden brown.
    5. Serve with blueberries and a drizzle of honey or maple syrup (optional).

**Recipe 5: Tuna Salad Stuffed Sweet Potatoes**

* **Prep Time:** 20 minutes
* **Ingredients:**
    * 2 Sweet Potatoes
    * 1 can Tuna (in water, drained)
    * 1/4 cup Greek Yogurt 
    * 1/4 cup Celery (chopped)
    * 1/4 cup Red Onion (diced)
    * 1 tbsp Olive Oil 
    * Salt and pepper to taste

* **Instructions:**
    1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Pierce sweet potatoes with a fork and rub with olive oil. Bake for 45-60 minutes, or until tender.
    2. While potatoes bake, prepare the tuna salad. Combine tuna, Greek yogurt, celery, red onion, salt, and pepper in a bowl.
    3. Once cooked, carefully cut the sweet potatoes open and fluff the flesh with a fork. 
    4. Fill each potato with a generous scoop of the tuna salad.
