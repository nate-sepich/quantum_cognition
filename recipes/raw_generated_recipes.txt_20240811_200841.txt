## 5 High-Protein, High-Carb Recipes (Under 30 Min Prep)

Here are 5 recipes designed to help you reach your macro goals, utilizing your current inventory and adding a few extra ingredients:

**Recipe 1: Chicken & Quinoa Bowl with Greek Yogurt Dressing**

* **Macros (approx.):** Protein: 192g, Carbs: 475g, Fat: 124g 
* **Prep time:** 25 minutes

**Ingredients:**

* 1 Chicken Breast (170g)
* ½ cup Quinoa (185g) 
* 1 cup Broccoli florets (91g)
* ½ cup Greek Yogurt (125g)
* 1 tbsp Olive Oil (14g)
* ½ tsp Dried Oregano
* Salt and Pepper to taste
* Optional: 1 tbsp Lemon juice for dressing

**Instructions:**

1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
2. Rinse quinoa and cook according to package directions. 
3. While quinoa cooks, season chicken breast with oregano, salt, and pepper.
4. Heat olive oil in an oven-safe skillet over medium heat. Sear chicken for 2 minutes per side. 
5. Add broccoli florets to the skillet around the chicken.
6. Transfer skillet to the preheated oven and bake for 15-20 minutes, or until chicken is cooked through.
7. While chicken bakes, whisk together Greek yogurt, lemon juice (optional), salt, and pepper for the dressing.
8. Once cooked, shred the chicken and combine with cooked quinoa and broccoli. Toss with dressing.

**Recipe 2: Salmon Fried Rice with Spinach and Almonds**

* **Macros (approx.):** Protein: 188g, Carbs: 479g, Fat: 127g
* **Prep time:** 25 minutes

**Ingredients:**

* 1 Salmon fillet (170g)
* 1 cup Brown Rice (cooked) (195g)
* 1 cup Spinach (67g)
* ¼ cup Almonds (30g)
* 1 tbsp Olive Oil (14g)
* 1 tbsp Soy Sauce
* 1 tsp Sesame Oil 
* ½ tsp Garlic powder
* Salt and Pepper to taste

**Instructions:**

1. Cook brown rice according to package directions. 
2. While rice cooks, season salmon with salt, pepper and garlic powder.
3. Heat olive oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. 
4. Pan-fry salmon for 3-4 minutes per side, or until cooked through. Remove from skillet and set aside.
5. In the same skillet, add cooked rice, spinach, and almonds. Stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until spinach wilts. 
6. Flake the cooked salmon into the rice mixture. 
7. Drizzle soy sauce and sesame oil over the rice. Stir-fry for another minute to combine all flavors. 

**Recipe 3: Greek Yogurt Breakfast Bowl with Berries and Nuts**

* **Macros (approx.):** Protein: 191g, Carbs: 476g, Fat: 125g
* **Prep time:** 15 minutes

**Ingredients:**

* 1 cup Greek Yogurt (250g) 
* ½ cup Rolled Oats (40g) - *Not in inventory, but readily available*
* ½ cup Mixed Berries (frozen or fresh) (140g)
* ¼ cup Almonds (30g)
* 2 tbsp Honey (50g) 
* ½ tsp Cinnamon

**Instructions:**

1. In a medium bowl, combine Greek yogurt, rolled oats, berries, almonds, honey, and cinnamon. 
2. Mix well to combine all ingredients. 
3. Let sit for 5 minutes to allow the oats to soften slightly, or enjoy immediately. 

**Recipe 4: Egg and Veggie Scramble with Whole Wheat Toast**

* **Macros (approx.):** Protein: 193g, Carbs: 478g, Fat: 124g
* **Prep time:** 20 minutes

**Ingredients:**

* 4 Eggs
* ½ cup Broccoli florets (91g)
* ½ cup Spinach (67g) 
* 1 tbsp Olive Oil (14g)
* 2 slices Whole Wheat Bread (64g) - *Not in inventory, but readily available*
* Salt and Pepper to taste

**Instructions:**

1. Whisk eggs in a bowl and season with salt and pepper. 
2. Heat olive oil in a nonstick skillet over medium heat.
3. Add broccoli florets and spinach to the skillet and sauté for 2-3 minutes until slightly softened. 
4. Pour in the whisked eggs and cook, stirring frequently, until eggs are set to your preference. 
5. While eggs are cooking, toast the bread slices.
6. Serve egg scramble alongside the toast.

**Recipe 5: Chicken Salad Stuffed Sweet Potatoes**

* **Macros (approx.):** Protein: 189g, Carbs: 475g, Fat: 128g
* **Prep time:** 25 minutes

**Ingredients:**

* 1 Chicken Breast (170g)
* 2 Medium Sweet Potatoes (300g) - *Not in inventory, but readily available*
* ¼ cup Greek Yogurt (62g)
* ¼ cup Celery (diced) (40g) - *Not in inventory, but readily available*
* 1 tbsp Olive Oil (14g) 
* Salt and Pepper to taste

**Instructions:**

1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Pierce sweet potatoes several times with a fork. Rub with olive oil and a pinch of salt. 
2. Place sweet potatoes directly on the oven rack and bake for 45-60 minutes, or until tender. 
3. While sweet potatoes bake, season and grill or pan-fry the chicken breast until cooked through. 
4. Once cooked, shred the chicken. 
5. In a bowl, combine shredded chicken, Greek yogurt, and diced celery. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
6. Once sweet potatoes are cooked, carefully slice them open lengthwise. 
7. Fill each sweet potato with the chicken salad.
