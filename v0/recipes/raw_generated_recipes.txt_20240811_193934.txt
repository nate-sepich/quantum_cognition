## 3 High-Protein Recipes (Under 30 Minutes)

Here are 3 recipes that utilize your current inventory and hit your macro goals, with a focus on quick preparation:

**Recipe 1: Chicken & Broccoli Fried Rice (Prep Time: 25 minutes)**

* **Macros (approx.):** Protein: 198g, Carbs: 472g, Fat: 121g
* **Ingredients:**
    * 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts (about 1 lb total)
    * 1 tbsp olive oil 
    * 1 large onion, diced
    * 2 cups cooked rice (white or brown)
    * 1 head broccoli, cut into florets
    * 4 large eggs
    * 2 tbsp soy sauce (low sodium preferred)
    * 1 tbsp sesame oil 
    * Salt and pepper to taste

* **Instructions:**
    1. Cut chicken breasts into bite-sized pieces. Season with salt and pepper.
    2. Heat olive oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add chicken and cook until browned and cooked through (about 5-7 minutes). Remove chicken from pan and set aside.
    3. Add onion to the pan and sauté until softened, about 3 minutes. Add broccoli florets and stir-fry for another 3-4 minutes.
    4. Push vegetables to one side of the pan. Crack eggs into the empty space and scramble until just set.
    5. Add cooked rice and chicken back to the pan. Stir everything together and heat through.
    6. Drizzle with soy sauce and sesame oil. Season with additional salt and pepper to taste.
    7. Serve immediately.

**Recipe 2: Cheesy Chicken & Rice Casserole (Prep Time: 20 minutes)**

* **Macros (approx.):** Protein: 185g, Carbs: 479g, Fat: 128g
* **Ingredients:**
    * 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts (about 1 lb total)
    * 1 tbsp olive oil
    * 1 (10.75 oz) can condensed cream of chicken soup 
    * 1 cup milk 
    * 2 cups cooked rice (white or brown) 
    * 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
    * Salt and pepper to taste 

* **Instructions:**
    1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C).
    2. Cut chicken breasts into bite-sized pieces. Season with salt and pepper.
    3. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add chicken and cook until browned and cooked through (about 5-7 minutes). 
    4. In a large bowl, combine cream of chicken soup, milk, 1/2 cup cheese, and salt and pepper to taste.
    5. Stir in cooked chicken and rice.
    6. Pour mixture into a greased 9x13 inch baking dish. Top with remaining cheese.
    7. Bake in preheated oven for 15-20 minutes, or until heated through and cheese is melted and bubbly.

**Recipe 3: Chicken Burrito Bowls with Quick Salsa (Prep Time: 25 minutes)**

* **Macros (approx.):** Protein: 192g, Carbs: 475g, Fat: 124g
* **Ingredients:**
    * 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts (about 1 lb total) 
    * 1 tbsp olive oil
    * 1 (15 oz) can black beans, rinsed and drained
    * 1 (15 oz) can corn, drained
    * 2 cups cooked rice (white or brown)
    * 1 cup salsa (store-bought or see quick recipe below)
    * 1/2 cup shredded cheddar cheese 
    * 1/4 cup sour cream (optional)

* **Quick Salsa Ingredients:**
    * 1 (14.5 oz) can diced tomatoes, undrained
    * 1/4 cup chopped onion
    * 1 small jalapeño, seeded and minced (optional)
    * 1 tbsp lime juice
    * Salt and pepper to taste

* **Instructions:**
    1. **For the Salsa (optional):** Combine all salsa ingredients in a bowl and mix well. Set aside.
    2. Cut chicken breasts into bite-sized pieces. Season with salt and pepper. 
    3. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add chicken and cook until browned and cooked through (about 5-7 minutes).
    4. To assemble bowls: Divide rice, black beans, corn, and chicken among four bowls. Top with salsa, cheese, and sour cream, if desired. 

**Note:** These recipes are estimates and can be adjusted to your liking. You can easily swap ingredients or adjust portion sizes to fine-tune the macros to your specific needs.
